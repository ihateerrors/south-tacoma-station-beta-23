Last year, we designated some of the proposed improvements as "Priority 1." These improvements best meet our three-point criteria of benefiting underserved communities, addressing a major barrier to access, and being located close to South Tacoma Station. The other proposed improvements were deemed “Priority 2.”

We are currently developing cost estimates for the Priority 1 and Priority 2 improvements, though these may change as we continue design work. We expect to construct as many Priority 1 improvements as we can before including Priority 2 improvements as funding allows.

Below are descriptions of the six Priority 2 improvements. Read on and learn how we expect to prioritize between these six proposals, then take our brief survey to let us know what you think. Each description has a summary of findings from our environmental review and a preliminary cost estimate.

Parking at South Tacoma Station

The Sound Transit 2 Plan (approved by voters in 2008) envisioned adding new parking stalls at South Tacoma Station, to meet expected demand. While the current 220 surface parking spaces at the station were close to capacity most weekdays in 2019, today they are only about 22% full on average, due to work-from-home and hybrid schedules.
